# CSE 110 Team Charter - Team 10

---

## 1. Purpose and Objectives

Our team exists to design, build, and deliver a high-quality software product over the course of CSE 110. Beyond the final deliverable, our purpose is to practice real-world software engineering — agile workflows, collaborative development, code review, testing, and clear documentation — in a way that prepares each member for industry.

**Objectives:**
- Ship a functional, well-tested, and documented application by the end of the quarter
- Practice and internalize agile development principles (sprints, standups, retrospectives)
- Build trust and communication habits that make the team more effective over time
- Ensure every member grows technically and professionally throughout the project

---

## 2. Values and Standards

We commit to the following core values as a team:

- **Respect** — Every member's time, ideas, and contributions are valued equally
- **Accountability** — We own our commitments and follow through on them
- **Transparency** — We communicate openly about blockers, availability, and disagreements
- **Quality** — We take pride in what we build; we don't ship work we aren't willing to put our name on
- **Growth** — We support each other in learning; no one is expected to know everything

## 4. Needs and Expectations

### Availability
- Each member is expected to contribute a minimum of **~4 hours per week** to the project
- Members should communicate scheduling conflicts **at least 48 hours in advance** when possible
- No one is expected to be available 24/7 — but responses to messages in Slack should happen within **24 hours on weekdays**

### Meeting Time
- The weekly team sync is held during our discussion period: **Mondays 4:00 – 5:00 PM**
- Members should attend in person or notify the team in advance if they cannot

### Collaboration Expectations
- Attend the weekly team sync or notify the team in advance if you cannot
- All code must go through a **pull request with at least one approving review** before merging to `main`
- Do not merge your own PRs without review except in documented emergencies

### Communication
- **Slack** is the primary async channel for team communication
- **GitHub Issues / Projects** is the source of truth for tasks and sprint work
- If something is urgent, tag or DM the relevant person directly

## 5. Conflict Resolution

We recognize that disagreements will happen. The following process outlines how we handle them:

1. **Direct conversation first** — If you have an issue with a teammate, address it with them directly and respectfully before escalating
2. **Team discussion** — If the issue is not resolved, bring it to the full team in the weekly sync for open discussion
3. **Team Lead mediation** — If still unresolved, a Team Lead will mediate and propose a resolution
4. **TA escalation** — If the conflict is serious or the Team Lead is involved, the team will escalate to the mentor TA

Dissenting opinions on technical or product decisions should be voiced during meetings. Once a decision is made by majority agreement, the team commits to it and moves forward.

---

## 6. Handling Rule Violations

If a team member is not meeting their commitments or following agreed-upon processes:

1. **First instance:** A friendly, private message from the Team Lead acknowledging the issue and offering support
2. **Second instance:** Discussion as a team — understanding root cause and adjusting workload or role if needed
3. **Repeated or serious violations:** Documented warning and escalation to the TA mentor
4. **Persistent non-participation:** TA and instructor are notified per course policy

We approach violations with the assumption of good intent first. The goal is to fix the problem, not punish the person.

## 7. Working Methods and Processes

- Tasks are tracked in **GitHub Projects** using a Kanban board (Backlog → In Progress → In Review → Done)
- All code lives in the `main` branch via PRs; feature branches should be named `feature/short-description`
- Commits should be meaningful and descriptive — avoid messages like "fix" or "stuff"
- We follow a **definition of done**: a task is complete when it is coded, tested, reviewed, merged, and documented

### Measuring Success
- Sprint velocity (tasks completed vs. planned)
- Test coverage maintained above 70%
- No unreviewed code on `main`
- TA feedback incorporated within one sprint
- Each member ships at least one PR per sprint

### Fair Contribution
- Tasks are assigned collaboratively during sprint planning — no one should feel overloaded or underutilized
- If a member's workload is unbalanced, they should raise it during the weekly sync
- At the end of each sprint, the team will do a brief check-in on workload distribution
